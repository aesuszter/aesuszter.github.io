Most discussions around AI security are either excessively optimistic for sales purposes or catastrophically vague and distopian due to a lack of insight. Neither is particularly useful. 

I work in penetration testing, which heavily shapes how I approach AI. When I look at such systems, I often see misplaced trust, superficial controls, and an expanded attack surface with vulnerabilities that may never get remediated by vendors.

This blog primarily focuses on AI security from an offensive security perspective, understanding systems' failure, misuse, and adversarial pressure. Some posts might also discuss more general patterns in behaviour observed both by myself and researchers in the field for optimizing operational AI interactions.

The field is evolving quickly, but many of its problems are already familiar. Complexity increases in the field, while visibility only ever decreases. Organisations deploy systems they do not fully understand, then attach them to business-critical environments.

This blog exists to inform about the behaviour and vulnerabilities of AI systems - I hope you will find it useful.
